---

## What Triggers the Need for This

In February 2026, a Southern District of New York judge sanctioned a firm for using an AI tool that accessed confidential client communications without adequate disclosure or consent. The ruling created three immediate exposure points for small firms:

1. **Privilege waiver risk** — undisclosed AI tool use touching client communications may constitute inadvertent waiver
2. **Vendor DPA gap** — most consumer and prosumer AI tools have no enforceable Data Processing Agreement suitable for attorney-client data
3. **Engagement letter deficiency** — standard engagement letters do not address AI use, creating informed consent gaps

The audit assesses your firm's current exposure on all three fronts and tells you exactly what to fix and in what order.

## Included in This Engagement

### Step 1: Pre-Audit Intake (Async, Before Call)
Client completes a written intake questionnaire covering:

- All AI and automation tools currently in use (by anyone in the firm — attorneys, paralegals, assistants)
- Existing vendor contracts or DPAs on file (if any)
- Current intake process (how a new lead becomes a client)
- Existing AI governance policy (if any)
- Current engagement letter (last version used)
- Current privilege log template or practice (if any)

Questionnaire is 12–15 questions. Estimated completion time: 30–45 minutes. Must be completed at least 48 hours before the intake call.

---

### Step 2: 90-Minute Intake Call
Live call with the managing partner (or equivalent decision-maker). Agenda:

1. Walk through questionnaire responses — clarify gaps (30 min)
2. Map actual AI tool usage against disclosed usage — identify shadow tools (20 min)
3. Describe current intake funnel in detail — identify drop points (20 min)
4. Identify top operational bottlenecks the managing partner perceives (20 min)

This call is recorded (with consent) for accuracy. Notes are not a substitute for review.

---

### Step 3: Audit Analysis (Internal, Not Billable to Client)
Following the intake call, the auditor reviews:

- All vendor terms of service and DPAs for disclosed AI tools (or absence thereof)
- Engagement letter language for AI disclosure and informed consent clauses
- Intake funnel description against known conversion benchmarks for practice area
- Privilege log practice against ABA Formal Opinion 512 requirements

Estimated internal time: 4–6 hours.

---

### Step 4: Written Report Delivery
**Deliverable:** A 5-page written PDF report structured as follows:

**Page 1 — AI Privilege Risk Rating**
- Overall rating: Red / Yellow / Green
- One-paragraph plain-English explanation of the rating
- Top 3 specific findings that drove the rating (cited, specific — e.g., "Firm uses [Tool X], which has no enterprise DPA and whose terms of service permit training on user inputs")

**Page 2 — Vendor DPA Status**
- Table: each AI tool in use, DPA status (Enterprise / Consumer-grade / None), and risk level
- Recommended action per tool (replace / upgrade / document / acceptable as-is)

**Page 3 — Intake Conversion Estimate**
- Estimated current intake conversion rate (lead to retained client) based on described process
- Comparison to practice-area benchmark range
- Top 2–3 identified drop points in the described funnel

**Page 4 — Top 3 Operational Bottlenecks**
- Each bottleneck stated as a specific, observable problem (not a category)
- Estimated impact (time lost, revenue at risk, or compliance exposure)

**Page 5 — Prioritized Fix List**
- All findings ranked by: (1) compliance urgency, (2) implementation effort, (3) revenue impact
- Each item tagged: Fix Immediately / Fix Within 90 Days / Fix Within 6 Months
- Each item tagged: DIY / Needs Outside Help
- Estimated effort range for each item (hours or weeks — not dollars, as those vary)

Report is delivered as a PDF via email. No verbal summary substitutes for the written report.

---

### Step 5: 60-Minute Debrief Call
Scheduled no earlier than 48 hours after report delivery (to allow reading time).

Agenda:
1. Walk through findings — clarify any questions on the report (20 min)
2. Discuss the Prioritized Fix List — confirm priorities with managing partner (20 min)
3. Explain which items BizDeedz can address via modules and which are DIY (10 min)
4. Answer questions (10 min)

This call does not extend into proposal or scoping for next steps. A separate follow-up is scheduled for that.

**Can we include a second attorney in the intake call?**
Yes. One additional attendee is permitted. The call is still 90 minutes. Additional attendees beyond two require pre-authorization and may extend the call with a corresponding scope adjustment.

**Can we get a copy of the recording?**
The recording is for BizDeedz internal accuracy purposes only and is not shared with the client.

**What if we disagree with a finding in the report?**
The debrief call is the appropriate venue for discussing findings. BizDeedz will explain the basis for each finding and will correct factual errors. Disagreements about judgment calls (e.g., risk level assessments) are noted in the record but do not result in a revised report.

**What happens after the debrief?**
BizDeedz will follow up within 3 business days with a separate module proposal based on the Prioritized Fix List. The client has no obligation to engage further.

**What if we already have a governance policy?**
The audit will review it. If it is materially compliant with ABA 512 and addresses the Heppner exposure, that will be reflected in the report. The audit fee does not change based on existing compliance status.
