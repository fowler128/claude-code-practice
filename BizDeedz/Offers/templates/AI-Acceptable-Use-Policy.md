## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the acceptable use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and services at [FIRM NAME]. It ensures AI is used in ways that enhance our practice while protecting client confidentiality, maintaining professional responsibility, and managing risk.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All attorneys, paralegals, and staff
- All AI tools, including but not limited to:
  - Generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.)
  - Practice-specific AI features in our software
  - Automation tools with AI components
  - Research and analytics tools using AI
- All work performed on behalf of the firm, including remote work

---

## 3. Definitions

**AI Tool:** Any software or service that uses machine learning, natural language processing, or similar technologies to generate, analyze, or process information.

**Public AI Tool:** AI services accessible to the general public where input may be used to train models or stored by third parties (e.g., free-tier ChatGPT, Bard).

**Enterprise AI Tool:** AI services with business agreements that include data protection, no training on inputs, and contractual safeguards.

**Approved AI Tool:** An AI tool vetted and authorized by the firm for specific use cases.

---

## 4. Guiding Principles

1. **Human Judgment First:** AI assists human decision-making; it does not replace professional judgment.

2. **Client Protection:** Client confidentiality and privilege must never be compromised.

3. **Accuracy Over Speed:** AI output must be verified before use; speed gains cannot come at the cost of correctness.

4. **Transparency:** When relevant, clients should be informed about AI use in their matters.

5. **Continuous Learning:** We will regularly review and update AI practices as technology and ethics evolve.

---

## 5. Approved Uses

The following uses of AI are **approved** when using vetted, enterprise AI tools:

### 5.1 Internal Productivity

- Summarizing internal documents and research
- Drafting internal communications
- Generating first drafts of administrative content
- Brainstorming and outlining

### 5.2 Legal Work (with Human Review)

| Activity | AI Role | Human Requirement |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| Document classification | Suggest category | Paralegal confirms |
| Template-based drafting | Generate first draft | Attorney reviews and edits |
| Deadline extraction | Identify dates from documents | Paralegal verifies |
| Research summarization | Summarize sources | Attorney validates |
| Client communication drafts | Generate draft text | Attorney approves before send |

### 5.3 Intake and Client Service

- Summarizing intake form responses
- Generating follow-up email drafts (human review required)
- Suggesting matter routing based on criteria
- Extracting key information from documents

---

## 6. Prohibited Uses

The following uses are **strictly prohibited**:

### 6.1 Public AI Tools with Client Data

**Never** input the following into public AI tools:
- Client names or identifying information
- Case details or facts
- Privileged communications
- Confidential documents or their contents
- Opposing party information
- Financial or billing data

### 6.2 Unsupervised Legal Work

- Generating legal advice sent directly to clients without attorney review
- Filing documents created by AI without human verification
- Making strategic decisions based solely on AI recommendations
- Using AI output as final work product

### 6.3 Misrepresentation

- Representing AI-generated content as original human work when disclosure is required
- Using AI to create false or misleading information
- Fabricating citations or authorities (AI "hallucinations")

### 6.4 Circumventing Controls

- Disabling logging or audit features
- Using personal accounts for firm work
- Sharing credentials for AI tools
- Using unapproved AI tools for client matters

## 7. Client Data Protection

### 7.1 Before Using AI with Client Information

1. **Confirm the tool is approved** for use with client data
2. **Verify data handling terms** (no training, encryption, deletion)
3. **Consider necessity:** Is AI needed, or is manual processing appropriate?
4. **Minimize data:** Use only the information required

### 7.2 Data Handling Requirements

| Data Type | Public AI | Enterprise AI (Approved) |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| Client PII | Never | With controls |
| Case facts | Never | With controls |
| Privileged content | Never | Carefully, with controls |
| Public information | Permitted | Permitted |
| Internal admin | Permitted | Permitted |

### 7.3 Engagement Letters

Consider including AI disclosure language in engagement letters:

> "Our firm may use artificial intelligence tools to assist with administrative tasks, document processing, and legal research. All AI-assisted work is reviewed by qualified attorneys. Client information is only used with AI tools that meet our data protection standards and is never used to train AI models."

## 8. Human Review Requirements

### 8.1 Mandatory Human Review

The following **always** require human review before use or transmission:

- Any communication sent to clients
- Any document filed with a court or agency
- Any legal advice or recommendations
- Any document sent to opposing counsel
- Any content containing legal citations
- Any work product leaving the firm

### 8.2 Review Standards

When reviewing AI output, the reviewer must:

1. **Verify accuracy:** Confirm facts, citations, and calculations
2. **Check for hallucinations:** Validate that cited cases/statutes exist
3. **Assess appropriateness:** Ensure tone and content fit the purpose

5. **Take ownership:** The reviewer is responsible for the final product
