## Article 1: Definitions

**1.1 "Agreement"** means this Master Service Agreement together with all Exhibits, Schedules, and Statements of Work incorporated herein.

**1.2 "Confidential Information"** means all non-public information disclosed by either party to the other, whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

**1.3 "Deliverables"** means all work product, documents, materials, and other tangible items to be delivered by Consultant under a Statement of Work.

**1.4 "Intellectual Property"** means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights.

**1.5 "Services"** means the consulting, implementation, training, and other services to be performed by Consultant under a Statement of Work.

**1.6 "Statement of Work" or "SOW"** means a document executed by both parties that describes the specific Services to be performed, Deliverables to be provided, timeline, and fees.

---

## Article 2: Engagement and Scope

**2.1 Engagement.** Company engages Consultant, and Consultant agrees to provide Services to Company, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and applicable Statements of Work.

**2.2 Statements of Work.** Each project or engagement shall be documented in a Statement of Work that references this Agreement. Each SOW shall specify:
- Description of Services
- Deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Fees and payment schedule
- Specific terms applicable to that engagement

**2.3 Order of Precedence.** In the event of conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of the SOW shall prevail with respect to that specific engagement, except for Articles 5 (Confidentiality), 7 (Limitation of Liability), and 9 (Indemnification), which may not be modified without express written amendment to this Agreement.

---

## Article 3: Performance of Services

**3.1 Standard of Care.** Consultant shall perform all Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards and practices.

**3.2 Personnel.** Consultant shall provide qualified personnel to perform the Services. Consultant may, at its discretion, use subcontractors to perform Services, provided that Consultant remains responsible for their performance and compliance with this Agreement.

**3.3 Independent Contractor.** Consultant is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties. Consultant shall be solely responsible for all taxes, insurance, and benefits relating to Consultant and its personnel.

**3.4 Company Cooperation.** Company shall:
- Provide timely access to personnel, systems, and information necessary for Consultant to perform Services
- Designate a primary contact with authority to make decisions
- Review and respond to Consultant deliverables within timeframes specified in the SOW
- Perform responsibilities assigned to Company in the SOW

**3.5 Delays.** If Company delays in performing its responsibilities, Consultant may adjust timelines accordingly. If delays exceed thirty (30) days, Consultant may invoice for work completed and suspend performance until delays are resolved.

---

## Article 4: Fees and Payment

**4.1 Fees.** Company shall pay Consultant the fees specified in each Statement of Work. Unless otherwise stated in a SOW:
- Fixed fee engagements are invoiced according to the milestone schedule
- Time and materials engagements are invoiced monthly in arrears
- Retainer engagements are invoiced monthly in advance

**4.2 Expenses.** Company shall reimburse Consultant for pre-approved, reasonable expenses incurred in performing Services, including travel, lodging, and third-party tools or services required for delivery.

**4.3 Payment Terms.** Invoices are due within thirty (30) days of receipt. Late payments accrue interest at one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

**4.4 Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of all taxes. Company is responsible for all applicable sales, use, VAT, or similar taxes, excluding taxes based on Consultant's income.

**4.5 Disputed Invoices.** Company must notify Consultant of any invoice disputes within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the disputed amount and reason. Undisputed portions remain due according to standard terms.

## Article 5: Confidentiality

**5.1 Obligations.** Each party agrees to:
- Hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement
- Disclose Confidential Information only to employees and contractors who need to know and are bound by similar confidentiality obligations
- Protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care

**5.2 Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party
- Was known to the receiving party before disclosure
- Is independently developed by the receiving party without use of Confidential Information
- Is rightfully obtained from a third party without confidentiality restrictions

**5.3 Required Disclosure.** A party may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, provided it gives the other party prompt notice (where legally permitted) to allow the other party to seek protective measures.

**5.4 Return of Information.** Upon termination or request, each party shall return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information, except for copies retained in accordance with legal or regulatory requirements.

**5.5 Duration.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

## Article 7: Limitation of Liability

**7.1 Exclusion of Damages.** NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOST DATA, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES WERE FORESEEABLE OR WHETHER A PARTY WAS ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**7.2 Cap on Liability.** EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF ARTICLE 5 (CONFIDENTIALITY) OR ARTICLE 9 (INDEMNIFICATION), EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY COMPANY UNDER THE APPLICABLE STATEMENT OF WORK, OR IF THE CLAIM DOES NOT RELATE TO A SPECIFIC SOW, THE TOTAL FEES PAID UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

**7.3 Basis of the Bargain.** The parties acknowledge that the limitations in this Article 7 are an essential basis of the bargain and reflect the allocation of risk between the parties.

## Article 9: Indemnification

**9.1 By Consultant.** Consultant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company from third-party claims alleging that Deliverables (excluding any Company materials or third-party materials) infringe such third party's Intellectual Property rights, provided Company:
- Gives prompt written notice of the claim
- Grants Consultant sole control of the defense and settlement
- Provides reasonable cooperation at Consultant's expense

**9.2 By Company.** Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Consultant from third-party claims arising from:
- Company's use of Deliverables in violation of this Agreement
- Company materials provided to Consultant
- Company's business operations unrelated to Consultant's Services

**9.3 Infringement Remedies.** If Deliverables are held or reasonably believed to infringe, Consultant may, at its option:
- Obtain the right for Company to continue using the Deliverable
- Modify the Deliverable to be non-infringing
- Replace the Deliverable with a non-infringing equivalent
- If none of the above are commercially reasonable, terminate the applicable SOW and refund fees paid for the infringing Deliverable

## Article 10: Term and Termination

**10.1 Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated as provided herein.

**10.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement by providing sixty (60) days written notice to the other party, provided that no SOW is then in effect. Active SOWs must be completed or separately terminated.

**10.3 Termination of SOW.** Either party may terminate an individual SOW by providing thirty (30) days written notice, subject to the payment obligations in Section 10.5.

**10.4 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if:
- The other party materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice
- The other party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases operations
- The other party fails to pay undisputed amounts within sixty (60) days of the due date

**10.5 Effect of Termination.**
- Company shall pay for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date
- Consultant shall deliver all completed and in-progress Deliverables upon payment
- Sections surviving termination: 4 (for accrued amounts), 5, 6, 7, 8.3, 9, and 11
