## 2) Why these are better than borderline leads

- They have **specific, dated trigger events** (funding rounds, spin-outs, strategic partnerships, or expansion announcements) rather than generic “growth” claims.
- Most sit in **ICP-aligned industries** (oil & gas tech, specialty construction tech, IT/AI, advanced manufacturing/energy).
- They show clearer **employer-side immigration use cases** (specialized technical hiring, cross-border expansion, new operational scale).
- Buyer paths are visible (People/Talent/Ops/Legal leaders), reducing risk of pure procurement dead-ends.

---

## 3) Red flags

- Defense/critical infrastructure overlaps can introduce export-control constraints for some roles.
- Some later-stage or PE-backed companies may have incumbent legal panels.
- If job postings signal broad “no sponsorship,” start with compliance/risk architecture rather than sponsorship-first outreach.
- Early-stage robotics firms may have high technical need but uneven process maturity.
